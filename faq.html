FAQ

Q. What is artificial intelligence?

A. Technically, a field of computer science dealing with the contruction of intelligent machines. Fine print:there isn't 100% consensus, even among computer scientists and engineers, about what defines AI. The thing is, once an AI technology has saturated the market, people come to think of it as the new normal, and not as an AI.1

Q. How smart are they really?

A. So AI come in 3 calibers2:

  1. Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI), aka Weak AI

    2. These AI can only perform a narrow set of designated tasks. They can't develop consciousness or ponder the meaning of life... they just do what their algorithms tells them to do, and spit out the results. It's kind of debatable whether AI at this level are actually "intelligent" (see: Chinese room argument).

    Examples: Google's self-driving car, Deep Blue, friend/product/song recommendations via Facebook, Amazon, Pandora, etc.

  2. Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), aka Strong AI

    3. AI at the human level--that is, these AI will be able to perform any intellectual tasks that a human can, and with as much ease. These AI will (theoretically) be self-aware, and have the ability to reason, plan, solve problems, think abstractly, learn quickly, and learn from experience.

    Examples: None, yet.

  3. Artificial Superintelligence (ASI)

    4. Superintelligent AI that trump human brains in just about every field. Will be wiser, more creative, and even more persuasive than us. Basically, the stuff of sci-fi movies.

    Examples: Her
